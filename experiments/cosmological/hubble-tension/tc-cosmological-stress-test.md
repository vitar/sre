# TC Cosmological Stress Test: Problems, Questions, Evidence

## Purpose

This document maps open cosmological problems to Tri-Contour Dynamics concepts, identifies the questions TC must answer to demonstrate explanatory value at cosmological scale, and defines the evidence that would validate or falsify TC's structural claims in this domain.

The exercise serves two directions simultaneously. **TC → Cosmology**: TC's structural logic generates predictions about cosmological phenomena that can be compared against observational data. **Cosmology → TC**: cosmological phenomena at extreme scales stress-test TC's generality claims, revealing boundary conditions and gaps that strengthen the model for all domains.

This is not a claim that TC replaces cosmological models. It is a claim that TC's structural logic — allocation under scarcity, competing contour demands, accumulation dynamics, mediator architecture — produces non-trivial, testable mappings when applied to a domain where scarcity, competition, and non-static behavior are present at the most extreme scales.

## Foundational Mapping

Before individual problems can be addressed, the contour mapping at cosmological scale must be established and defended.

### Contour Definitions at Cosmological Scale

**Survival** — maintaining structural integrity against entropy. Gravity binding matter into structures, nuclear forces holding atoms, dark energy maintaining spacetime fabric. Functional role: preserve what exists.

**Reproduction** — copying and scaling existing patterns. Star formation following established nucleosynthetic pathways, galaxies assembling from gravitational templates, matter replicating known configurations across space. Functional role: produce more of what works.

**Evolution** — changing form and capability. Supernovae forging new elements, phase transitions creating new states of matter, emergence of structures that did not previously exist. Functional role: produce what the system was not previously capable of.

**Resource** — energy/matter, conserved per region, governed by conservation laws. Scarcity is enforced by thermodynamic limits and the finite energy budget of any bounded region.

**Time** — the medium through which allocation is enacted, subject to relativistic frame dependence (addressed by Metabolic Frame Dependence in System Metabolism).

### Questions to Validate the Mapping

Q1. **Do the three contours produce genuine trade-offs at cosmological scale?** The model applies only where allocation to one contour reduces what is available to others. Evidence required: demonstrable cases where energy/matter committed to structural maintenance (S) is unavailable for star formation (R) or element creation (E), and vice versa.

Q2. **Is the contour distinction analytically useful or merely relabeling?** The mapping must produce insights that the existing cosmological vocabulary does not. If "Survival = gravity" and "Reproduction = star formation" add no explanatory value beyond renaming, the mapping fails. Evidence required: at least one prediction or diagnostic that TC's contour logic generates which standard cosmological framing does not naturally produce.

Q3. **Are mediators identifiable and structurally distinct from contours?** Dark matter is proposed as a mediator — shaping allocation without competing for the same resource pool. This must be defended: does dark matter genuinely operate cross-cuttingly (shaping where matter flows without itself being allocated among S/R/E), or does it participate in contour competition directly? Evidence required: demonstration that dark matter's role is better described as Gate configuration than as contour allocation.

## Problem 1: Hubble Tension

### The Cosmological Problem

Two independent measurements of the universe's expansion rate produce incompatible results. The CMB-based measurement (Planck satellite, modeling initial conditions forward) yields ~67 km/s/Mpc. The distance-ladder measurement (SH0ES team, measuring current expansion directly) yields ~73 km/s/Mpc. The 8-9% gap exceeds measurement uncertainty and persists after JWST verification.

### TC Mapping

**Code-Narrative Divergence.** The CMB measurement derives from initial conditions (the universe's founding Code) projected forward through the standard model's equations (Narrative). The distance-ladder measurement observes current metabolic output directly. The gap between them is structurally analogous to Code-Narrative Divergence: the model (Narrative) predicts one metabolic rate, but the system's actual operating logic (Code) produces another.

**Alternative mapping: Mediator Reconfiguration.** If the divergence is caused not by a Narrative error but by an actual change in the system's mediator architecture between 380,000 years and now, the forward projection would systematically underestimate the current rate — because it assumes static mediator configuration.

### Questions TC Must Answer

Q4. **Which TC mechanism produces the divergence?** Code-Narrative Divergence (the model is wrong) and Mediator Reconfiguration (the system changed) are structurally different diagnoses with different intervention implications. TC must specify which mechanism it predicts, or define the observational criteria that would distinguish between them.

Q5. **If Mediator Reconfiguration, what changed and when?** TC predicts that a mediator change between measurement epochs would produce compound effects across multiple observables. This must be specified: which mediator property changed, what accumulation dynamic drove the change, and what the predicted temporal profile of the change is.

### TC Prediction (Testable)

**P1. The Hubble tension and S8 tension should resolve together.** TC's structural logic: when two apparently independent anomalies both point toward the same contour being over-allocated (Survival overhead displacing Reproduction), they are symptoms of a single distortion, not independent problems. Any model that resolves one tension should also resolve the other. If they resolve independently through unrelated mechanisms, TC's "single distortion, multiple symptoms" claim is weakened for this domain.

**P2. If the cause is Mediator Reconfiguration, the reconfiguration should have a temporal profile consistent with TC's accumulation dynamics.** Specifically: if dark matter properties drifted gradually (Code drift), the divergence should correlate with epoch — measurements from different cosmic eras should show systematically different expansion rates, not a single step-change. If the change was an Endogenous Phase Transition (threshold crossing), there should be evidence of a discontinuous shift at a specific epoch.

### Evidence That Would Validate TC

- Future instruments (Roman Space Telescope, Euclid) resolving both tensions simultaneously through a single mechanism, consistent with P1.
- Evidence of epoch-dependent dark matter behavior, consistent with mediator drift (P2).
- A model incorporating time-varying dark matter properties that resolves the Hubble tension also resolving S8, without independent tuning for each.

### Evidence That Would Challenge TC

- The two tensions resolving through entirely unrelated mechanisms (different physics, different epochs, no shared cause).
- The tension resolving through a measurement error that has no structural analog in TC (pure instrumental artifact with no systemic interpretation).

## Problem 2: S8 Tension

### The Cosmological Problem

The standard model predicts a specific amount of matter clustering across space. Direct measurements consistently find slightly less clustering than predicted. Adam Riess has called it "the little sibling of the Hubble tension."

### TC Mapping

**Survival Overhead Displacing Reproduction.** Dark energy (68% of the universe's energy budget) maps to Survival overhead — the allocation share claimed by maintaining spacetime fabric. The S8 tension (less structure formation than predicted) maps to under-performing Reproduction — less clustering, less structure, less scaling of existing patterns. TC reads this as: Survival overhead is crowding out Reproduction allocation.

### Questions TC Must Answer

Q6. **Is dark energy's allocation share increasing, stable, or decreasing?** TC's overhead ratchet predicts that once Survival allocation crosses a threshold, it locks into an escalating regime. If dark energy is the cosmological analog of Survival overhead, its share should be increasing — consistent with accelerating expansion. If dark energy's share is constant (cosmological constant), the ratchet mechanism does not apply and TC must explain why overhead in this system does not exhibit threshold-regime dynamics.

Q7. **Does the degree of S8 under-clustering correlate with the Hubble tension magnitude?** TC predicts compound symptoms from single distortion. The correlation should be quantifiable: regions or epochs with higher expansion rate should show proportionally less clustering.

### TC Prediction (Testable)

**P3. Dark energy should exhibit threshold-regime behavior.** If dark energy is Survival overhead, its behavior should show a transition from proportional-and-reversible to locked-and-escalating at some identifiable cosmic epoch — consistent with the observation that accelerating expansion "turned on" approximately 5 billion years ago.

**P4. The S8 deficit should scale with dark energy dominance.** As dark energy's share of the universe's energy budget increased over cosmic time, the deficit between predicted and observed clustering should have grown proportionally. Earlier epochs (when dark energy was a smaller share) should show less S8 tension.

### Evidence That Would Validate TC

- Observations confirming that dark energy's equation of state changed at a specific epoch (threshold crossing), rather than being constant throughout cosmic history.
- Correlation between dark energy dominance fraction and clustering deficit across different cosmic epochs.

### Evidence That Would Challenge TC

- Dark energy being exactly a cosmological constant with no temporal variation (no threshold, no ratchet, no regime change).
- S8 tension being fully explained by baryonic physics (gas dynamics, feedback processes) with no connection to dark energy behavior.

## Problem 3: Too-Massive Early Galaxies

### The Cosmological Problem

JWST found galaxies at 500-700 million years after the Big Bang that rival the Milky Way in mass. Standard models predict ~10% star formation efficiency; these galaxies appear to approach 100% — converting nearly all available gas into stars. After correction for black hole luminosity contamination ("little red dots"), roughly twice as many massive galaxies remain as the standard model expects.

### TC Mapping

**Boundary Dissolution Window.** The early universe at 500-700 Myr was in extreme environmental conditions — high density, high radiation, high temperature. TC predicts that under sufficient systemic stress, normal Gate constraints are temporarily suspended (Boundary Dissolution). The usual 10% star formation efficiency represents normal Gate selectivity — most gas is filtered out by radiation pressure, thermal dynamics, and turbulence. During a Boundary Dissolution window, these Gate constraints are suspended, allowing matter to flow into structures without normal filtering.

**Proxy Discipline Failure (little red dots).** The initial overestimate of galaxy masses — caused by black hole luminosity being misread as stellar mass — is a direct proxy discipline failure. Brightness was used as a proxy for mass without declaring the conditions under which the proxy becomes unreliable (active galactic nuclei producing luminosity disproportionate to stellar mass).

### Questions TC Must Answer

Q8. **Can TC predict the duration and conditions of the Dissolution window?** If Boundary Dissolution explains the elevated star formation efficiency, TC must specify what determines the window's duration: when did normal Gate control reconstitute, and what triggered reconstitution? In TC's framework, enforcement reconstitutes "often more aggressively than before." Is there evidence that post-window star formation efficiency dropped below the normal 10% baseline?

Q9. **Does Boundary Dissolution predict structural signatures in dissolution-era galaxies?** TC predicts that systems formed during dissolution lack normal internal Gate architecture — less differentiated structure, less hierarchical organization. Do early massive galaxies show structural characteristics consistent with formation under suspended selectivity?

### TC Prediction (Testable)

**P5. Galaxies formed during the Dissolution window should show less internal structural differentiation than galaxies formed after the window closed.** Specifically: less morphological diversity within the galaxy (more uniform composition), less hierarchical substructure, and potentially a more sudden transition to structured morphology once the window closes — consistent with post-dissolution enforcement reconstitution.

**P6. Star formation efficiency should show a non-monotonic profile across cosmic time.** Not a smooth decline from early high efficiency to current low efficiency, but a peak during the dissolution window followed by a dip below baseline (post-dissolution enforcement overshoot), then a gradual return to the current ~10% regime.

### Evidence That Would Validate TC

- Morphological studies showing that early massive galaxies have less internal substructure than same-mass galaxies formed later.
- Star formation rate measurements across cosmic time showing a post-peak undershoot before settling to current rates.
- The corrected (post-"little red dots") galaxy mass function showing a sharp transition rather than a smooth curve — consistent with a window opening and closing, not a gradual parameter shift.

### Evidence That Would Challenge TC

- Early massive galaxies showing the same internal structural complexity as later galaxies (no dissolution signature).
- Star formation efficiency declining smoothly and monotonically across cosmic time (no window, no overshoot).

## Problem 4: Dark Matter as Mediator

### The Cosmological Problem

Dark matter (27% of the universe) does not interact electromagnetically — it does not emit, absorb, or reflect light. But it shapes gravitational structure: where galaxies form, how they cluster, how gravitational lensing bends light. Its nature is unknown — cold dark matter (heavy, slow particles), warm dark matter (lighter, faster), and wave dark matter (ultralight, quantum-scale wavelength) produce different structural predictions.

JWST observations of elongated ("cigar-shaped") early galaxies match warm/wave dark matter predictions better than cold dark matter predictions.

### TC Mapping

**Mediator Architecture.** Dark matter is proposed as a TC mediator — a cross-cutting factor that shapes allocation without competing for the same resource pool as the three contours. It configures Gates: determining where matter can flow (gravitational wells), what structures are permitted to form (halo mass functions), and what large-scale patterns emerge (cosmic web filaments).

The cold vs. warm vs. wave debate maps to a debate about **mediator configuration**: are the Gates sharp and localized (cold → clumpy, compact structures) or smooth and distributed (warm/wave → elongated filaments, smoother flow)?

### Questions TC Must Answer

Q10. **Is the mediator mapping structurally valid?** Dark matter must satisfy TC's mediator criteria: it shapes allocation dynamics without itself being allocated among the three contours. If dark matter participates directly in contour competition (e.g., dark matter halos contributing to Survival by gravitationally binding structures), it may be a contour participant, not a mediator. The boundary between "shaping allocation" and "participating in allocation" must be drawn precisely.

Q11. **Does TC's mediator framework predict which dark matter model is correct?** If dark matter is a mediator configuring Gates, TC's structural logic may constrain which Gate configuration is consistent with observed allocation outcomes. Specifically: does the observed balance of structure formation (Reproduction output) and structural diversity (Evolution output) at various cosmic epochs constrain the mediator to sharp Gates (cold) or smooth Gates (warm/wave)?

Q12. **Can mediator reconfiguration over time explain the Hubble tension?** If dark matter's mediator properties are not constant — if Gate configuration has drifted over cosmic time — this could produce the divergence between early-universe predictions and current observations. TC's Code drift mechanism provides the structural pathway: the mediator's operating logic accumulates incremental change through repeated small departures.

### TC Prediction (Testable)

**P7. If dark matter is a mediator with smooth Gate configuration (warm/wave), the transition from dissolution-era to post-dissolution-era structure should be gradual, not sharp.** Smooth Gates produce gradual changes in flow patterns. Sharp Gates produce abrupt transitions. The morphological transition of galaxies across cosmic time should discriminate between mediator configurations.

**P8. Mediator reconfiguration should produce correlated effects across multiple observables simultaneously.** TC predicts that mediator changes affect all allocation dynamics, not just one. If dark matter properties changed, the effects should appear in clustering (S8), expansion rate (Hubble tension), galaxy morphology, and gravitational lensing — not in isolation. Observations finding correlated anomalies across these domains would support mediator reconfiguration; isolated anomalies in only one domain would challenge it.

### Evidence That Would Validate TC

- Confirmation that dark matter properties vary with cosmic epoch (mediator drift), producing correlated anomalies across multiple observational domains.
- Elongated galaxy morphologies at high redshift transitioning gradually to compact morphologies at lower redshift, consistent with smooth Gate reconfiguration.

### Evidence That Would Challenge TC

- Dark matter being definitively cold (sharp Gates) while early galaxies show smooth, elongated morphology — contradicting the prediction that Gate configuration determines structural outcome.
- Dark matter properties being rigorously constant across all cosmic epochs, eliminating mediator drift as an explanatory mechanism.

## Problem 5: Dark Energy as Survival Overhead

### The Cosmological Problem

Dark energy constitutes 68% of the universe's energy budget. It drives accelerating expansion. Its nature is unknown — it may be a cosmological constant (fixed energy density of empty space), a dynamic field that changes over time, or a property of spacetime itself. The accelerating expansion was discovered in 1998 and appears to have become dominant approximately 5 billion years ago.

### TC Mapping

**Survival Overhead with Ratchet Dynamics.** Dark energy maps to the metabolic cost of maintaining spacetime fabric — the allocation share claimed by structural maintenance of the universe itself. TC's overhead dynamics predict threshold-regime behavior: below a critical threshold, overhead is proportional and reversible; above it, overhead locks into an escalating regime that persists and grows.

The observation that dark energy "turned on" (became dominant) approximately 5 billion years ago is consistent with a ratchet crossing — the universe's Survival overhead crossed a threshold and locked into an escalating regime.

### Questions TC Must Answer

Q13. **Does the cosmological constant scenario falsify TC's overhead mapping?** If dark energy is a true cosmological constant — fixed, unchanging, present since the beginning — it does not exhibit threshold-regime behavior. TC must either explain why Survival overhead in this system does not ratchet (an exception to the model's dynamics), or predict that dark energy is not constant and will be shown to vary.

Q14. **What is the threshold mechanism?** TC's overhead ratchet in human systems is driven by Code incorporation — crisis-era processes become embedded in operating logic. What is the cosmological analog? What "Code" incorporated elevated overhead as normal? If TC cannot identify the mechanism, the mapping is metaphorical rather than structural.

Q15. **Does TC predict a terminal trajectory?** If dark energy is escalating Survival overhead, TC's model predicts eventual crowding out of Reproduction and Evolution entirely — a trajectory toward Contour Saturation. Cosmologically, this maps to heat death: expansion so fast that no new structures form (no Reproduction) and no new physical processes emerge (no Evolution). Does TC's Saturation → Reconstitution pathway predict anything about the universe's ultimate fate that standard cosmology does not?

### TC Prediction (Testable)

**P9. Dark energy should not be exactly a cosmological constant.** TC's overhead dynamics predict variation — specifically, threshold-regime behavior with at least one identifiable transition epoch. Recent evidence from DESI (Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument) suggesting time-varying dark energy would be consistent with this prediction.

**P10. If dark energy is escalating, the rate of escalation should follow TC's overhead curve shape — not linear, but threshold-regime with ratchet.** This means: a period of proportional behavior, a transition at a specific density/expansion threshold, and a post-transition regime that persists independently of the original trigger.

### Evidence That Would Validate TC

- DESI or successor surveys confirming time-varying dark energy with identifiable regime transitions.
- The dark energy equation of state showing non-linear temporal behavior consistent with threshold-regime dynamics rather than smooth evolution.

### Evidence That Would Challenge TC

- Definitive confirmation that dark energy is exactly a cosmological constant with no variation across cosmic time.
- Dark energy variation that follows a smooth, continuous curve with no threshold or regime transition — inconsistent with ratchet dynamics.

## Problem 6: Black Holes as Contour Saturation

### The Cosmological Problem

Black holes represent extreme gravitational binding — regions where spacetime curvature becomes so severe that nothing, including light, can escape. At the event horizon, time dilation approaches infinity (from an external frame). Hawking radiation provides a mechanism for extremely slow mass loss. The interior structure and information fate remain unresolved.

### TC Mapping

**Contour Saturation.** A black hole is the state where Survival allocation reaches the system's physical limit. All resources (mass-energy) are bound to structural maintenance (gravitational binding). Reproduction allocation is zero (nothing propagates outward). Evolution allocation is zero (no structural change communicates outward). This is TC's Contour Saturation: one contour consuming the entire allocation space, eliminating degrees of freedom for the other two.

**Hawking Radiation as Balancing Feedback.** TC predicts that Contour Saturation cannot persist permanently — the displaced contours' demands persist at nonzero levels, generating unbounded displacement pressure. Hawking radiation is the balancing feedback mechanism: quantum effects at the horizon slowly drain the Survival allocation, converting bound mass-energy back into radiation (resources returning to the environment). The feedback is extraordinarily slow, but it exists.

**Time dilation as Metabolic Frame Dependence.** The event horizon is where Metabolic Frame Dependence becomes maximally consequential. From an external frame, the black hole's metabolism asymptotically approaches zero observable rate. From a frame falling inward, metabolic activity continues. The same system presents radically different apparent metabolic states depending on the observer's frame — the exact condition Metabolic Frame Dependence describes.

### Questions TC Must Answer

Q16. **Does Saturation predict anything about black hole interiors?** TC's Saturation mechanism says the allocation logic ceases to function — the system is no longer allocating, it is locked. Does this structural claim map to any physical prediction about the black hole interior that differs from general relativity's predictions?

Q17. **Does Hawking radiation's rate follow TC's feedback dynamics?** TC predicts that balancing feedback strength is proportional to displacement pressure. If Hawking radiation is balancing feedback against Survival Saturation, its rate should be related to the degree of Saturation (mass of the black hole). Hawking radiation is inversely proportional to mass — smaller black holes radiate faster. Does TC's feedback logic predict this inverse relationship, or does it predict something different?

Q18. **Is the event horizon a Boundary in TC's structural sense?** If so, Hawking radiation is a flow across that Boundary — a Gate-mediated resource transfer from inside (the black hole) to outside (the environment). What configures this Gate? What determines its selectivity?

### TC Prediction (Testable)

**P11. Hawking radiation should exhibit properties consistent with TC's balancing feedback — specifically, it should counteract the dominant contour's allocation.** The radiation should carry energy (reducing Survival allocation) in a form that is available for Reproduction or Evolution in the external environment. This is consistent with Hawking radiation being thermal radiation — undifferentiated energy available for any use.

**P12. If black holes eventually evaporate completely, TC predicts the endpoint is Reconstitution, not annihilation.** The black hole's resources disperse, its Boundary dissolves, and its deep Code (the physical laws governing the mass-energy it contained) persists in the successor state. The evaporation endpoint should return energy to the environment in a form governed by the same physical laws — consistent with the conservation principles that constitute deep Code.

### Evidence That Would Validate TC

- Hawking radiation (if ever detected) being thermal and undifferentiated — consistent with resources returning to the environment without contour-specific commitment.
- Black hole evaporation endpoints preserving information (unitarity) — consistent with deep Code persistence through Reconstitution.

### Evidence That Would Challenge TC

- Hawking radiation carrying structured information (not undifferentiated energy) — suggesting Reproduction or Evolution contour activity at the horizon, contradicting the Saturation claim of zero R and E allocation.
- Information loss in black hole evaporation — contradicting Code persistence.

## Problem 7: Big Bang as Reconstitution

### The Cosmological Problem

The universe began from an extremely hot, dense state approximately 13.8 billion years ago. The initial conditions encoded in the CMB show remarkable uniformity with tiny fluctuations that seeded all subsequent structure. What preceded the Big Bang (if anything) and why the initial conditions took their specific form are open questions. Cyclic cosmology models propose that the Big Bang was not a unique event but one in a series of expansion-contraction-expansion cycles.

### TC Mapping

**Collapse → Reconstitution with Code Persistence.** A prior system reached terminal Survival-dominant accumulation (Contour Saturation in the form of gravitational collapse to a singularity). All resources were bound in structural maintenance. The displaced contours' demands accumulated without bound. The system reached Collapse — Boundary failure, resource dispersal, cessation of system operation. What followed was Reconstitution: resources reorganized into a new system under new allocation conditions, governed by persisting deep Code (physical constants, conservation laws).

**Code Persistence across Reconstitution.** Physical laws (conservation of energy, quantum mechanics, general relativity) constitute deep Code — foundational operating logic not dependent on any specific allocation pattern. They persist through the Collapse-Reconstitution transition because the failure was allocation-driven (Survival consumed everything), not Code-driven (the laws themselves did not fail). The new system inherits this Code but begins with a reset allocation pattern — the uniform, hot, dense initial state represents a fresh allocation with maximum degrees of freedom.

### Questions TC Must Answer

Q19. **Is the singularity a genuine Contour Saturation state?** The Big Bang singularity (if it is physically real and not a mathematical artifact) represents a state of maximum energy density in minimum volume. Does this satisfy Saturation's three conditions: allocation collapse (all resources bound to one contour), demand persistence on displaced contours (R and E demands nonzero), and compensation impossibility (no buffer available)?

Q20. **What drove the Reconstitution?** TC's Saturation exit dynamic says displaced contours' demands accumulate without bound until internal pressure exceeds structural containment capacity. Cosmologically, what is the "pressure" from unmet Reproduction and Evolution demands in a singularity? Is there a physical analog, or is the mapping metaphorical at this point?

Q21. **Does Code drift across cycles predict observable consequences?** If the universe is cyclic, TC predicts that deep Code (physical constants) is subject to drift across cycles — each Collapse-Reconstitution event potentially modifying which Code fragments persist and how they are activated. Do physical constants vary slightly across cycles? Is this testable?

Q22. **Does the allocation reset predict the CMB uniformity?** TC says Reconstitution resets allocation — the successor system does not inherit the prior system's contour posture. The CMB's remarkable uniformity (plus tiny fluctuations) could be read as a nearly complete allocation reset with minimal residual structure from the prior cycle. Can TC predict the degree of uniformity (how complete the reset is) from its structural logic?

### TC Prediction (Testable)

**P13. If the universe is cyclic, each cycle should be governed by recognizably similar but not identical deep Code.** Physical constants should show variation across cycles (if cycles are detectable), following TC's Code drift dynamics — incremental, not random. This is extremely difficult to test with current observational capabilities but constitutes a falsifiable structural claim.

**P14. The initial allocation pattern (immediately post-Big Bang) should show maximum degrees of freedom — all three contours available for allocation, no single contour dominant.** The hot, uniform, radiation-dominated early universe is consistent with this: energy distributed uniformly, no structures yet locked into Survival maintenance, no replication patterns yet established, maximum potential for structural Evolution. This is consistent with TC's Reconstitution claim of allocation reset.

### Evidence That Would Validate TC

- Cyclic cosmology models gaining observational support (e.g., patterns in the CMB consistent with pre-Big Bang structure — as some researchers have claimed but not confirmed).
- The initial post-Big Bang state being demonstrably the highest-entropy configuration available under the governing physical laws — consistent with maximum allocation degrees of freedom.

### Evidence That Would Challenge TC

- The Big Bang singularity being a genuine physical state rather than a mathematical artifact, with no physical mechanism corresponding to TC's "displacement pressure exceeding containment" — making the Saturation exit dynamic physically unmapped.
- Evidence that physical constants are rigorously identical across cosmic time with no mechanism for variation — eliminating Code drift as a meaningful concept at this scale.

## Problem 8: Early Dark Energy

### The Cosmological Problem

Early dark energy is a hypothetical burst of extra expansion in the first few hundred thousand years after the Big Bang, which then vanished. If it occurred, it would shift the Planck prediction upward toward 73 without breaking other standard model predictions. No direct detection exists.

### TC Mapping

**Endogenous Phase Transition.** Early dark energy maps to an Endogenous Phase Transition — a discontinuous allocation shift triggered by the system's internal state crossing a critical threshold, not by external environmental change. In the early universe, internal energy density and temperature were evolving continuously; at a specific threshold, the allocation regime shifted (a burst of Survival overhead), then shifted back when the threshold was no longer exceeded.

This is distinct from a permanent ratchet (Problem 5). Early dark energy is a transient Endogenous Phase Transition — the allocation regime crossed a threshold, shifted, and returned. TC's dynamics allow for this: not all threshold crossings produce permanent ratchets. If the threshold is narrow (the conditions that triggered the shift are transient), the system can exit the shifted regime when conditions change.

### Questions TC Must Answer

Q23. **Can TC distinguish transient from permanent Endogenous Phase Transitions?** The model needs to specify what determines whether a threshold crossing produces a permanent regime shift (ratchet) or a transient one (early dark energy). In human systems, the distinction is Code incorporation — the shift becomes permanent when operating logic embeds it. What is the cosmological analog?

Q24. **Does TC predict the timing and magnitude of early dark energy?** If EPT is the mechanism, TC's accumulation dynamics should constrain when the threshold was crossed (what accumulation level triggers it) and how large the resulting allocation shift was. Can these be derived from TC's structural logic, or are they free parameters?

### TC Prediction (Testable)

**P15. If early dark energy was a transient EPT, its temporal profile should be asymmetric — rapid onset, gradual decay.** TC's threshold dynamics predict a discontinuous shift at the trigger point (rapid onset) followed by a gradual return as the conditions that produced the threshold state change (gradual decay). The profile should not be symmetric (gradual onset, gradual decay) or step-function (instant on, instant off).

### Evidence That Would Validate TC

- Detection of early dark energy with an asymmetric temporal profile — rapid onset, gradual decay.
- The magnitude of early dark energy correlating with the specific energy density threshold at its epoch — consistent with threshold-triggered transition rather than arbitrary injection.

### Evidence That Would Challenge TC

- Early dark energy with a symmetric or step-function profile — inconsistent with TC's threshold-onset dynamics.
- Early dark energy requiring fine-tuning to a specific magnitude with no structural reason for that value — suggesting a free parameter rather than a threshold-derived quantity.

## Cross-Cutting Validation Criteria

Beyond individual problems, TC's value at cosmological scale depends on structural properties that span multiple problems.

### Criterion 1: Internal Consistency

TC's mappings across the eight problems must be mutually consistent. If dark energy is Survival overhead (Problem 5), and the Hubble tension is caused by overhead displacing Reproduction (Problem 1), and the S8 tension is the Reproduction-side symptom of the same displacement (Problem 2), these three mappings must produce compatible quantitative predictions. Internal contradiction between mappings would indicate that the contour definitions are inconsistent at cosmological scale.

### Criterion 2: Non-Trivial Predictions

TC must produce predictions that are not already obvious from standard cosmological reasoning. If every TC prediction is a restatement of an existing cosmological expectation in different vocabulary, the model adds no value. At least one prediction (ideally several) must be genuinely novel — something a cosmologist would not have predicted without TC's structural logic.

**Strongest candidate for non-trivial prediction:** P1 (Hubble and S8 tensions resolving together through a single mechanism). This is a structural prediction from TC's "single distortion, multiple symptoms" logic. Some cosmologists have noted the possible connection (Riess calling S8 the "little sibling"), but TC provides a structural reason why they must be connected, not merely a suggestion that they might be.

**Second candidate:** P6 (star formation efficiency showing a non-monotonic profile with post-dissolution overshoot). This is a specific temporal prediction that standard models of smooth efficiency evolution do not generate.

### Criterion 3: Falsifiability

Every TC prediction in this document must be falsifiable. If a prediction cannot be tested against observational data (even in principle), it is not evidence for TC — it is speculation. The predictions are organized by testability:

**Testable with current or near-term data:** P1 (Hubble/S8 correlation), P3 (dark energy temporal variation — DESI data), P4 (S8 scaling with dark energy dominance), P9 (dark energy not exactly constant).

**Testable with next-generation instruments:** P5 (dissolution-era galaxy morphology), P6 (star formation efficiency temporal profile), P7 (morphological transition gradual vs. sharp), P8 (correlated anomalies across observational domains), P10 (dark energy ratchet curve shape), P15 (early dark energy asymmetric profile).

**Testable in principle but not with foreseeable instruments:** P11 (Hawking radiation properties), P12 (black hole evaporation endpoints), P13 (physical constant variation across cycles), P14 (post-Big Bang allocation degrees of freedom).

### Criterion 4: Bidirectional Value

The exercise must strengthen TC, not just apply it. The four model extensions discovered through this stress test (Contour Saturation, Endogenous Phase Transition, Reconstitution, Metabolic Frame Dependence) must prove valuable in TC's primary domain (human systems), not only in cosmology. If the extensions are cosmology-specific with no organizational application, they add complexity without breadth.

**Preliminary assessment:** all four extensions have clear organizational analogs identified during development. Contour Saturation (monopolistic resource capture), Endogenous Phase Transition (internal complexity threshold causing spontaneous reorganization), Reconstitution (post-bankruptcy successor organizations), Metabolic Frame Dependence (board vs. engineering perspective disagreements). The extensions were discovered through cosmological stress testing but are not cosmology-specific.
