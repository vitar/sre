# Execution 01 — Problem 1: Organism Boundary Legitimacy

## Scope
Problem tested: **Is the organism a legitimate system boundary?**

## Decision
The individual organism is a defensible primary boundary when environment and dependency conditions are explicitly declared.

## Case ID
BIO-4-001

## Biological context
Breeding adult passerine bird during seasonal food fluctuation.

## Unit boundary
Individual organism (female bird), with nesting context declared.

## Time horizon
Pre-breeding through chick provisioning window.

## Environment
Variable food density, predation pressure, ambient temperature variability.

## Resource constraints
Energy intake, foraging time, immune capacity, thermal reserve.

## Observed demands
Maintenance, anti-predator vigilance, egg production, chick provisioning.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: thermoregulation, immune maintenance, anti-predator behavior.
- Reproduction: egg production, mating, parental provisioning.
- Evolution: foraging strategy adjustment and behavior restructuring for future conditions.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (genetic + endocrine regulation), Boundary (organism body), Gate (behavioral and hormonal gating of effort), Signal (food/predator/social cues), Receiver (sensory-endocrine-neural processing).

## Mediators
Stress hormones, circadian state, dominance position, weather volatility.

## What supports TC
- Boundary remains coherent across the period.
- Reallocation decisions are organism-level, not reducible to one tissue.
- Changing boundary to colony/population changes diagnosis materially.

## What challenges TC
- Strong social dependence can blur organism vs pair-level allocation.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** organism as a valid bounded unit for this case.
