# Execution 09 — Problem 9: Level Discipline (Organism vs Sub-organism vs Population)

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does analysis remain at organism level without level slippage?**

## Decision
Yes, if explanation rules are explicit: organism-level allocation claims must not be justified only by tissue-level mechanism or population-level adaptive storytelling.

## Case ID
BIO-4-009

## Biological context
Trait response often narrated as "evolutionary advantage" but expressed through individual allocation under stress.

## Unit boundary
Individual organism, with supporting lower/higher-level data separated.

## Time horizon
Single breeding season plus carryover effects.

## Environment
Ecological pressure with competition and disease exposure.

## Resource constraints
Energy, immune capacity, mating opportunity, developmental timing.

## Observed demands
Defense/repair, reproduction, adaptive behavior.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: immediate organism viability demands.
- Reproduction: breeding effort and offspring investment.
- Evolution: within-lifetime capability shift affecting future actions.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (organism regulatory architecture), Boundary (individual body), Gate (physiological/behavioral filters), Signal (ecological/social cues), Receiver (organism perception and response systems).

## Mediators
State-dependent endocrine and immune context, developmental stage.

## What supports TC
- Keeping analytical levels separate improves diagnostic clarity.
- Organism-level contour decisions remain interpretable without conflating species-level selection.

## What challenges TC
- Many biological narratives default to population-level explanation, tempting category drift.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** with explicit level-separation rules.
