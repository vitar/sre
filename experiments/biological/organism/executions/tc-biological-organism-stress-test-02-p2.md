# Execution 02 — Problem 2: Contour Distinguishability

## Scope
Problem tested: **Are Survival, Reproduction, and Evolution distinguishable at organism level?**

## Decision
Contours are distinguishable using primary functional intent, even with overlapping effects.

## Case ID
BIO-4-002

## Biological context
Mammal under intermittent stress with observed reproductive suppression.

## Unit boundary
Individual organism.

## Time horizon
One breeding cycle under stress episodes.

## Environment
Food moderate, elevated disturbance/predation cues, variable shelter quality.

## Resource constraints
Metabolic energy, endocrine bandwidth, immune-repair capacity, mating opportunity.

## Observed demands
Homeostasis, mating effort, behavioral adaptation to risk.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: stress response, immune activation, defensive behavior.
- Reproduction: mating, gamete-related investment, sexual signaling.
- Evolution: durable behavioral adaptation improving later option space.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (neuroendocrine program), Boundary (body), Gate (hormonal checkpoints), Signal (threat and mating cues), Receiver (state-dependent neural/endocrine interpretation).

## Mediators
Glucocorticoid tone, developmental stage, social status.

## What supports TC
- Stress-triggered reproductive suppression is Survival-heavy.
- Mating effort trade-offs clearly reduce safety margin.
- Persistent learning/adaptation differs from immediate maintenance and breeding.

## What challenges TC
- Short-term plasticity can look Evolution-like unless persistence criteria are enforced.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** with classification guardrails.
