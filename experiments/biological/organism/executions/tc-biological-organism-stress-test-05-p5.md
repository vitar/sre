# Execution 05 — Problem 5: Discipline of Organism-Level Evolution

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does TC Evolution remain narrow and useful at organism level?**

## Decision
It is usable when restricted to changes that alter future possibility space beyond immediate reversible state shifts.

## Case ID
BIO-4-005

## Biological context
Juvenile-to-adult maturation plus learned foraging specialization.

## Unit boundary
Individual organism.

## Time horizon
Ontogenetic transition and early adult phase.

## Environment
Resource heterogeneity, predation gradients, social competition.

## Resource constraints
Learning time, neural plasticity cost, energy budget, exposure risk.

## Observed demands
Maintenance, reproductive readiness, capability-building.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: immediate protection and homeostasis.
- Reproduction: maturation-linked reproductive function.
- Evolution: persistent learning/developmental commitment that changes future action set.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (developmental/neural plasticity constraints), Boundary (organism), Gate (sensitive periods and behavioral thresholds), Signal (ecological feedback), Receiver (perception-learning system).

## Mediators
Developmental stage, hormone profile, stress history.

## What supports TC
- Durable learning and maturation alter long-term opportunity space.
- Reversible short-term stress responses can be excluded from Evolution category.

## What challenges TC
- Without explicit persistence criteria, Evolution risks becoming “any adaptive change.”

## Confidence
Medium-high

## Verdict
**Partially supports** with strict definitional constraints.
