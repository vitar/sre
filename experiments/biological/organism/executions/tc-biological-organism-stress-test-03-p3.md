# Execution 03 — Problem 3: Scarcity-Driven Tension

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does scarcity create real contour tension?**

## Decision
Yes. Energetic and temporal scarcity produce measurable reallocation among contours.

## Case ID
BIO-4-003

## Biological context
Small mammal during prolonged caloric restriction across reproductive season.

## Unit boundary
Individual organism.

## Time horizon
Late pre-breeding to post-breeding period.

## Environment
Low food abundance, temperature stress, intermittent pathogen exposure.

## Resource constraints
Calories, micronutrients, immune budget, active time.

## Observed demands
Thermoregulation, infection control, reproductive effort, offspring care.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: energy conservation, immune defense, thermal maintenance.
- Reproduction: gamete production, mating, gestation/lactation effort.
- Evolution: adaptive behavior change (foraging schedule/risk strategy) with future payoff.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (energy/endocrine regulation), Boundary (organism), Gate (metabolic and behavioral priorities), Signal (food, pathogen, climate cues), Receiver (physiological sensing + CNS integration).

## Mediators
Leptin/cortisol-like state signals, disease burden, season timing.

## What supports TC
- Scarcity suppresses reproductive output and shifts toward maintenance.
- Immune activation reduces growth/reproductive allocation.
- Posture shifts are time-varying, not static.

## What challenges TC
- In mild scarcity windows, contour tension may be subtle.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports**: scarcity generates real and trackable contour competition.
