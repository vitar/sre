# Execution 06 — Problem 6: Mediators at Organism Level

## Scope
Problem tested: **Are mediators visible and necessary without becoming a fourth contour?**

## Decision
Yes. Cross-cutting organism states redirect contour allocation and signal processing.

## Case ID
BIO-4-006

## Biological context
Same species individuals exposed to similar conditions but different stress endocrine states.

## Unit boundary
Individual organism (comparative cases).

## Time horizon
Matched window across one reproductive phase.

## Environment
Equivalent food and habitat, different prior stress exposure.

## Resource constraints
Energy and endocrine bandwidth, immune reserve, mating opportunity.

## Observed demands
Maintenance, reproduction, behavioral adaptation.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
Survival/Reproduction/Evolution as shared baseline; focus is mediator-induced allocation differences.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (shared species baseline + individual state), Boundary (organism), Gate (state-dependent endocrine/behavioral access), Signal (same external cues), Receiver (differential sensitivity by mediator state).

## Mediators
Stress hormones, immune tone, circadian alignment, developmental phase.

## What supports TC
- Same cue produces different allocation outcomes across mediator states.
- Mediators change gating/thresholds without requiring new contour category.

## What challenges TC
- If mediator is defined too loosely, it collapses into generic “context.”

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** mediator necessity and specificity.
