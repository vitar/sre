# Execution 07 — Problem 7: Time as Constitutive at Organism Scale

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does time materially alter interpretation?**

## Decision
Yes. Stage, accumulation, and delay effects change meaning of identical present-state observations.

## Case ID
BIO-4-007

## Biological context
Repeated short-term stress reallocations across lifespan segment.

## Unit boundary
Individual organism longitudinally tracked.

## Time horizon
Early adulthood through post-reproductive onset.

## Environment
Fluctuating resource and pathogen pressure.

## Resource constraints
Cumulative repair budget, endocrine recovery capacity, reproductive window length.

## Observed demands
Acute maintenance, reproductive episodes, adaptation over repeated cycles.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: repeated crisis management.
- Reproduction: periodic reproductive investment.
- Evolution: accumulated capability shifts and behavioral restructuring.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (history-dependent physiological state), Boundary (organism continuity), Gate (age/stage-dependent thresholds), Signal (recurring stress cues), Receiver (changing sensitivity over time).

## Mediators
Aging state, chronic inflammation load, hormonal drift.

## What supports TC
- Same current posture can mean resilience early and debt-laden fragility later.
- Repeated reallocations produce delayed visible outcomes.
- Snapshot-only analysis misses debt accumulation.

## What challenges TC
- Requires longitudinal evidence; cross-sectional data can misclassify state.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** strong temporal constitutiveness.
