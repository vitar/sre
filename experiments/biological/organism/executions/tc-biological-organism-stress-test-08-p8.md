# Execution 08 — Problem 8: Cross-check with Life History Theory

## Scope
Problem tested: **Can TC be cross-checked with Life History Theory (LHT) without disappearing into it?**

## Decision
LHT provides strong corroboration of trade-off structure; TC adds distinct diagnostic structure when element and mediator logic are used explicitly.

## Case ID
BIO-4-008

## Biological context
Organism-level maintenance-growth-reproduction trade-offs under scarcity.

## Unit boundary
Individual organism in declared ecology.

## Time horizon
Multi-stage (development + reproductive window).

## Environment
Variable food, predation risk, and seasonality.

## Resource constraints
Energy, time, immune capacity, mating access.

## Observed demands
Maintenance, reproductive output, developmental/behavioral capability change.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival ↔ maintenance/homeostasis.
- Reproduction ↔ fecundity investment.
- Evolution ↔ organism-level capability-changing transformation.

## Structural elements mapping
Code/Boundary/Gate/Signal/Receiver operationalized through endocrine-neural-immune and behavioral mechanisms.

## Mediators
Hormonal status, stress burden, developmental stage, social rank.

## What supports TC
- LHT-like trade-offs align strongly with contour competition.
- TC contributes boundary discipline, gate/receiver legibility, and mediator-sensitive diagnosis.

## What challenges TC
- If only trade-off vocabulary is used, TC adds little beyond LHT.

## Confidence
Medium-high

## Verdict
**Partially supports**: corroborative but not substitutive relationship with LHT.
