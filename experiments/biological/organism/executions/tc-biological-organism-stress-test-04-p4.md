# Execution 04 — Problem 4: Structural Element Mapping

## Scope
Problem tested: **Can Code/Boundary/Gate/Signal/Receiver be mapped concretely?**

## Decision
Yes; all five map to observable organism mechanisms with diagnostic relevance.

## Case ID
BIO-4-004

## Biological context
Seasonal breeder with state-dependent reproductive activation.

## Unit boundary
Individual organism across seasonal transition.

## Time horizon
Non-breeding to breeding onset.

## Environment
Photoperiod change, resource pulse timing, social cue variation.

## Resource constraints
Energy reserve, endocrine readiness, immune reserve, mating window.

## Observed demands
Maintenance, reproductive activation, preparatory behavioral restructuring.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: homeostasis and risk management.
- Reproduction: gonadal activation, mating behavior.
- Evolution: seasonal behavioral/cognitive adaptation that alters future performance.

## Structural elements mapping
- Code: genetic-developmental-endocrine response architecture.
- Boundary: organism physiological boundary.
- Gate: endocrine checkpointing and behavioral access control.
- Signal: photoperiod, social, predator, and nutritional cues.
- Receiver: sensory and endocrine-neural systems with thresholds.

## Mediators
Circadian/circannual timing, stress endocrine state, immune status.

## What supports TC
- Same signal yields different outputs by organism state (receiver/gate effects).
- Threshold behavior is observable in reproductive activation.
- Mapping increases explanatory precision versus generic adaptation language.

## What challenges TC
- Some gates are distributed and difficult to localize as a single mechanism.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** strong concrete mapping.
