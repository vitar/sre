# Execution 02 — Problem 2: Contour Distinguishability

## Scope
Problem tested: **Are Survival, Reproduction, Evolution distinguishable?**

## Decision
Contours are distinguishable by **primary demand**, despite overlapping downstream effects.

## Case ID
BIO-1-002

## Biological context
Neural progenitor cells under variable proliferation and differentiation cues.

## Unit boundary
Lineage segment within developing neural tissue.

## Environment
Mitogen-rich vs differentiation-promoting signaling, oxidative stress variation.

## Resource constraints
Energy and redox budget, ribosomal/protein synthesis capacity, replication competence, time in developmental window.

## Observed demands
Repair and homeostasis, cell-cycle progression, terminal differentiation.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: ROS handling, DNA repair, viability programs.
- Reproduction: progenitor expansion through mitosis.
- Evolution: differentiation to neuron/glia with potency loss.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (GRN/epigenetics), Boundary (cell + lineage), Gate (cell-cycle and fate checkpoints), Signal (Notch/Wnt/BMP-like cues), Receiver (thresholded pathway integration).

## Mediators
Local morphogen context, checkpoint intensity, niche density.

## What supports TC
- Repair-induced cycle pause is a clear Survival shift.
- Proliferative expansion without commitment is Reproduction-heavy.
- Commitment with reduced proliferative potential is Evolution-heavy.

## What challenges TC
- Some biosynthetic activity serves all three contours simultaneously; requires primary-function rule to avoid forced labeling.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** with partial overlap acknowledged.
