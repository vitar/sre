# Execution 01 — Problem 1: Unit Boundary Legitimacy

## Scope
Problem tested: **Is the unit boundary legitimate?**

## Decision
Use **stem/progenitor cell lineage in a defined tissue niche** as primary unit. Single-cell-only framing is insufficient for allocation control across time.

## Case ID
BIO-1-001

## Biological context
Hematopoietic stem/progenitor lineage balancing quiescence, self-renewal, and commitment.

## Unit boundary
**Primary:** lineage-in-niche subsystem.  
**Alternative tested:** isolated single HSC.

## Environment
Nutrient and oxygen variability, cytokine signaling, inflammation/stress cues, staged developmental timing.

## Resource constraints
ATP/NAD(P)H budget, biosynthetic substrates, DNA repair bandwidth, receptor occupancy, niche access.

## Observed demands
Viability maintenance, proliferation, differentiation commitment, checkpoint repair.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: quiescence, repair, anti-apoptotic maintenance.
- Reproduction: self-renewing division.
- Evolution: lineage commitment/differentiation altering future possibility space.

## Structural elements mapping
- Code: gene-regulatory + epigenetic state controlling fate responses.
- Boundary: membrane + lineage identity constraints.
- Gate: receptor repertoire/checkpoints controlling entry into cycle or fate pathways.
- Signal: cytokines, growth factors, stress ligands.
- Receiver: receptor-pathway-transcription coupling and thresholds.

## Mediators
Niche architecture, cytokine milieu, checkpoint regulators, developmental timing windows.

## What supports TC
- Boundary choice changes diagnosis predictably: isolated cell view over-attributes autonomy; lineage-in-niche view captures real allocation control.
- Inside/outside is meaningful at both membrane and lineage-identity levels.
- Signal admission is selective and state dependent.

## What challenges TC
- Boundary is multi-layered (cell membrane, lineage identity, niche compartment), risking analytic ambiguity unless explicitly declared.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** Problem 1 confirmation signal with a declared, defensible boundary.
