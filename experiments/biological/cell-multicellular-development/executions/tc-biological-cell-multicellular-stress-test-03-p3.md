# Execution 03 — Problem 3: Scarcity and Real Tension

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does scarcity create contour tension?**

## Decision
Yes. Scarcity drives measurable reallocation among maintenance, proliferation, and differentiation.

## Case ID
BIO-1-003

## Biological context
Developing epithelial lineage under nutrient limitation and stress.

## Unit boundary
Tissue-local lineage cluster.

## Environment
Reduced nutrients, intermittent oxidative stress, variable growth-factor access.

## Resource constraints
ATP, amino acids, lipid precursors, repair enzyme capacity, spatial access.

## Observed demands
Stress defense, proliferation, maturation.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: stress response + autophagy + repair.
- Reproduction: cell-cycle entry and division.
- Evolution: maturation/specialization transitions.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (stress-response and fate programs), Boundary (cell/tissue compartment), Gate (nutrient transport + cycle checkpoints), Signal (stress and growth cues), Receiver (state-dependent pathway response).

## Mediators
Checkpoint activation, local competition for nutrients/signals.

## What supports TC
- Stress-induced cell-cycle arrest diverts allocation from Reproduction to Survival.
- Nutrient limitation delays differentiation and growth simultaneously, exposing real competition.
- Fate outcomes differ by signal access under positional scarcity.

## What challenges TC
- In mild scarcity, tension may appear weak; strong inference requires clear scarcity conditions.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports**: scarcity generates nontrivial contour tension.
