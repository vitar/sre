# Execution 07 — Problem 7: Time as Constitutive

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does time materially change interpretation (not just description)?**

## Decision
Yes. Developmental time changes meaning of identical signals/states and constrains later possibilities.

## Case ID
BIO-1-007

## Biological context
Lineage progression across competence windows and commitment thresholds.

## Unit boundary
Developmental lineage segment tracked longitudinally.

## Environment
Stage-dependent signaling contexts over explicit timepoints.

## Resource constraints
Time-windowed receptor competence, cumulative repair debt, gradual epigenetic priming.

## Observed demands
Viability, expansion, commitment.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: preserving viability and integrity over delays.
- Reproduction: expansion when cycle-permissive.
- Evolution: staged transition once threshold crossed.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (time-accumulated state), Boundary (lineage identity over time), Gate (windowed access), Signal (same cue with time-varying effect), Receiver (competence-dependent response).

## Mediators
Developmental clock, checkpoint timing, cumulative signaling history.

## What supports TC
- Early allocations constrain later fate options (path dependence).
- Seemingly abrupt commitment often follows gradual preparatory accumulation.
- Same signal can be inert early, decisive later.

## What challenges TC
- Sparse temporal sampling can falsely imply sudden transitions.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** strong temporal constitutiveness.
