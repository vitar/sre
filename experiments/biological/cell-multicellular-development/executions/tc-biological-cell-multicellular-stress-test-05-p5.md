# Execution 05 — Problem 5: TC “Evolution” in Development

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does TC Evolution (capability-changing transformation) remain usable here?**

## Decision
Yes, if constrained to **state transitions that alter future possibility space** (e.g., commitment and potency loss), not “any change.”

## Case ID
BIO-1-005

## Biological context
Stem-cell commitment to differentiated lineage.

## Unit boundary
Stem/progenitor lineage over staged development.

## Environment
Differentiation cues plus developmental timing constraints.

## Resource constraints
Epigenetic remodeling bandwidth, transcriptional capacity, metabolic support for transition.

## Observed demands
Maintenance of viability, division, and irreversible commitment.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: maintenance during transition stress.
- Reproduction: self-renewal divisions pre-commitment.
- Evolution: commitment/maturation causing capability shift and reduced potency.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (rewired regulatory programs), Boundary (identity boundary shift), Gate (commitment checkpoints), Signal (fate cues), Receiver (competence-dependent interpretation).

## Mediators
Timing of competence windows, niche cues, checkpoint strictness.

## What supports TC
- Differentiation clearly changes capability set and future options.
- Commitment events are not well described as copying (Reproduction) or maintenance (Survival).

## What challenges TC
- Programmed transitions risk over-broad “Evolution” labeling unless irreversibility/capability criteria are enforced.

## Confidence
Medium-high

## Verdict
**Partially supports** with strict definition guardrails.
