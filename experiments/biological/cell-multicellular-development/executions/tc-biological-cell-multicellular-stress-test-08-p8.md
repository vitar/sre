# Execution 08 — Problem 8: Cross-check with Life History Theory (LHT)

## Scope
Problem tested: **Can TC be cross-checked against LHT without distortion?**

## Decision
Cross-check is useful as **supporting correspondence**, not derivation. TC remains coherent at cell/development scale with careful scale discipline.

## Case ID
BIO-1-008

## Biological context
Maintenance-growth-reproduction trade-offs translated to cell/development analogs.

## Unit boundary
Lineage/tissue developmental subsystem (not species/population level).

## Environment
Variable stress, nutrient, and signaling availability over developmental stages.

## Resource constraints
Energetic and regulatory budgets allocated across maintenance, expansion, transformation.

## Observed demands
Repair/homeostasis, proliferative output, differentiation and specialization.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival ↔ maintenance.
- Reproduction ↔ proliferative copying.
- Evolution ↔ capability-changing developmental transformation.

## Structural elements mapping
Code/Boundary/Gate/Signal/Receiver remain defined by cellular-regulatory architecture.

## Mediators
Niche and timing context that modulate trade-off expression.

## What supports TC
- Trade-off language aligns with many biological descriptions of maintenance vs growth/reproduction.
- TC adds structural diagnostics (elements + mediators + boundary discipline) beyond simple trade-off naming.

## What challenges TC
- LHT is often population/organism scale; naive import causes scale slippage.
- Over-reliance on LHT vocabulary can mask weak TC-specific mapping.

## Confidence
Medium-high

## Verdict
**Partially supports**: cross-check strengthens fit when scale boundaries are explicit.
