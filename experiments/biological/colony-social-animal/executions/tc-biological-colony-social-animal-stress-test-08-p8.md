# Execution 08 — Problem 8: Discipline of TC Evolution at Group Level

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does TC Evolution remain disciplined at colony/social-unit level?**

## Decision
Yes, if limited to persistent capability-changing collective transformations, not transient behavioral fluctuations.

## Case ID
BIO-3-008

## Biological context
Social bird colony adopting durable cooperative defense and foraging pattern shifts.

## Unit boundary
Breeding colony.

## Time horizon
Five breeding seasons.

## Environment
Predator regime shift and resource unpredictability.

## Resource constraints
Foraging windows, vigilance labor, nesting-site quality.

## Observed demands
Current persistence, reproductive output, longer-term coordination adaptation.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: immediate risk management and cohesion.
- Reproduction: sustained breeding success.
- Evolution: persistent coordination and communication restructuring improving future capability.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (colony coordination schema), Boundary (membership), Gate (role-access and participation filters), Signal (alarm/social coordination cues), Receiver (subgroup-specific decoding).

## Mediators
Leadership distribution, predation intensity, colony density.

## Multi-unit dynamics observed
Adaptive restructuring redistributed load across subgroups and changed future response capacity.

## What supports TC
- Distinguishes durable transformation from short-term reactive adjustments.

## What challenges TC
- Without persistence threshold, category drifts into “any group change.”

## Confidence
Medium-high

## Verdict
**Partially supports** with strict criteria.
