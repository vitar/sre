# Execution 02 — Problem 2: Contour Distinguishability at Collective Level

## Scope
Problem tested: **Are Survival, Reproduction, Evolution distinguishable at collective level?**

## Decision
Yes, when primary functional intent is used and ambiguous events are deferred.

## Case ID
BIO-3-002

## Biological context
Cooperative breeder mammal group with variable litter suppression.

## Unit boundary
Single social group.

## Time horizon
Two breeding cycles.

## Environment
Food fluctuation, predator pressure, territorial conflict.

## Resource constraints
Foraging yield, helper labor, safe den access, breeding opportunities.

## Observed demands
Group defense, breeding output control, social-role adjustment.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: collective defense and cohesion maintenance.
- Reproduction: breeding effort and offspring survival.
- Evolution: persistent coordination/role changes improving future group options.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (group social protocol), Boundary (membership recognition), Gate (dominance-mediated breeding access), Signal (alarm/social cues), Receiver (status- and role-dependent responders).

## Mediators
Dominance hierarchy, helper availability, predation regime.

## Multi-unit dynamics observed
Local breeder benefit can conflict with group maintenance demand.

## What supports TC
- Breeding suppression under stress is clearly Survival-heavy at group scale.
- Role reallocations can be separated from immediate breeding output.

## What challenges TC
- Some social behaviors serve two contours simultaneously and need explicit primary-function selection.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** with disciplined classification rules.
