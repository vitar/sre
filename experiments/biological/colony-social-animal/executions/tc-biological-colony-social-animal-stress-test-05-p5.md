# Execution 05 — Problem 5: Value of Multi-Unit TC Dynamics

## Scope
Problem tested: **Do multi-unit TC dynamics add real explanatory power?**

## Decision
Yes. Compensation, cascade, and decoupling explain group outcomes better than flat single-unit summaries.

## Case ID
BIO-3-005

## Biological context
Wolf pack with breeder loss and subsequent role redistribution.

## Unit boundary
Single pack.

## Time horizon
Pre-loss, disruption, and recovery period.

## Environment
Seasonal prey variation, territorial competition.

## Resource constraints
Hunting success, pup care effort, territorial defense labor.

## Observed demands
Pack maintenance, reproduction continuity, strategy adaptation.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: pack persistence and hunt stability.
- Reproduction: successful pup raising and breeder replacement dynamics.
- Evolution: durable role restructuring and hunting coordination changes.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (pack coordination rules), Boundary (pack membership), Gate (role admission and breeding access), Signal (coordination and threat cues), Receiver (role-dependent responders).

## Mediators
Leadership structure, prey distribution, social cohesion strength.

## Multi-unit dynamics observed
- Cross-unit compensation by subadults.
- Cascade risk when specialist roles fail.
- Temporary local viability masking collective fragility.

## What supports TC
- Multi-unit lens predicts delayed instability despite short-term functional compensation.

## What challenges TC
- Requires high-resolution group interaction data to distinguish compensation from randomness.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** clear added value of multi-unit logic.
