# Execution 04 — Problem 4: Structural Element Mapping

## Scope
Problem tested: **Can Code/Boundary/Gate/Signal/Receiver map concretely?**

## Decision
Yes. Social systems provide concrete mechanisms for all five TC elements.

## Case ID
BIO-3-004

## Biological context
Primate troop with hierarchy-mediated access and signaling.

## Unit boundary
Single troop.

## Time horizon
Multi-season social cycle.

## Environment
Variable food patches, social competition, predation risk.

## Resource constraints
Food access time, mate access, coalition support, vigilance bandwidth.

## Observed demands
Group cohesion, reproductive control, adaptive alliance/role adjustments.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: troop cohesion and risk mitigation.
- Reproduction: mating opportunity distribution and offspring support.
- Evolution: durable social-structure and coordination changes.

## Structural elements mapping
- Code: social norms, recognition patterns, role expectations.
- Boundary: membership and outsider exclusion.
- Gate: hierarchy-based access to mates/resources/positions.
- Signal: vocal/gesture displays, alarm calls, dominance cues.
- Receiver: role-specific and status-specific signal interpretation.

## Mediators
Dominance hierarchy, alliance network topology, ecological volatility.

## Multi-unit dynamics observed
Feedback asymmetry: high-rank signals propagate faster than low-rank signals.

## What supports TC
- Signal legibility and gate control are directly observable.
- Same signal can produce different outcomes by receiver status.

## What challenges TC
- Multiple overlapping gate types complicate single-gate attribution.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** strong element-level mapping.
