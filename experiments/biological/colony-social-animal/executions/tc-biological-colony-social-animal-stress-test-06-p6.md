# Execution 06 — Problem 6: Mediators at Colony/Social Scale

## Scope
Problem tested: **Are mediators visible and necessary at this level?**

## Decision
Yes. Hierarchy and communication architecture act as mediators shaping allocation and feedback.

## Case ID
BIO-3-006

## Biological context
Meerkat group under fluctuating predation and helper turnover.

## Unit boundary
Single cooperative group.

## Time horizon
Three breeding seasons.

## Environment
Predator volatility, rainfall variability, competitive neighboring groups.

## Resource constraints
Sentinel labor, pup-care time, foraging access, safe den capacity.

## Observed demands
Defense, reproduction, group reorganization.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: sentinel defense and group safety.
- Reproduction: pup production and successful rearing.
- Evolution: persistent role-coordination shifts improving resilience.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (group behavioral protocol), Boundary (group membership), Gate (role/admission control), Signal (alarm/contact calls), Receiver (age- and role-dependent responders).

## Mediators
Dominance status, sentinel network quality, territory pressure.

## Multi-unit dynamics observed
Mediator shifts alter feedback speed and who bears compensatory load.

## What supports TC
- Same ecological cue yields different allocations under different mediator configurations.
- Mediators shape contours without needing separate contour status.

## What challenges TC
- Over-broad mediator labeling can collapse into generic context description.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** mediator specificity and necessity.
