# Execution 01 — Problem 1: Collective Boundary Legitimacy

## Scope
Problem tested: **Is the colony/social unit a legitimate system boundary?**

## Decision
A coordinated social unit (not a loose aggregation) is a defensible collective boundary when membership and role continuity are explicit.

## Case ID
BIO-3-001

## Biological context
Honeybee colony under seasonal forage stress.

## Unit boundary
Single colony (queen, workers, brood, nest infrastructure).

## Time horizon
One full seasonal cycle.

## Environment
Declining forage window, pesticide stress, variable temperature.

## Resource constraints
Nectar/pollen inflow, worker labor capacity, brood care bandwidth, thermoregulation effort.

## Observed demands
Colony maintenance, brood production, adaptive foraging reallocation.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: colony thermoregulation, disease control, overwinter viability.
- Reproduction: brood production and successful next-generation emergence.
- Evolution: sustained role/task reconfiguration improving future colony capability.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (social-role and colony behavior rules), Boundary (colony membership + nest), Gate (admission and role-access controls), Signal (pheromone/forage/alarm cues), Receiver (caste-specific response systems).

## Mediators
Queen status signals, forage landscape, disease load.

## Multi-unit dynamics observed
Cross-unit compensation: workers absorb reproductive cost to stabilize collective output.

## What supports TC
- Colony persists despite individual turnover, indicating true collective continuity.
- Collective diagnosis differs from any single worker diagnosis.

## What challenges TC
- Boundary can blur during swarming/splitting phases unless phase is explicitly scoped.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** defensible collective boundary.
