# Execution 07 — Problem 7: Time as Constitutive in Social Units

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does time behave the way TC requires?**

## Decision
Yes. Hidden strain, seasonal phase shifts, and delayed breakdown are structurally time-dependent.

## Case ID
BIO-3-007

## Biological context
Colony showing apparent seasonal success before abrupt collapse.

## Unit boundary
Single colony monitored longitudinally.

## Time horizon
Multi-season trajectory including collapse phase.

## Environment
Intermittent resource shocks, pathogen pulses, weather extremes.

## Resource constraints
Labor reserve, brood-care capacity, disease defense bandwidth.

## Observed demands
Maintenance, brood continuity, adaptive behavior changes.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: preserving colony integrity in each season.
- Reproduction: maintaining brood cycle output.
- Evolution: accumulated coordination adaptations affecting future robustness.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (history-dependent social state), Boundary (colony continuity), Gate (phase-dependent role activation), Signal (seasonal/pathogen cues), Receiver (caste-specific time-sensitive response).

## Mediators
Season timing, disease burden, age-structure drift.

## Multi-unit dynamics observed
Long compensation period masked structural debt before visible failure.

## What supports TC
- Snapshot interpretation would miss cumulative pre-collapse trajectory.

## What challenges TC
- Weak temporal sampling can falsely imply sudden unexplained collapse.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** strong temporal constitutiveness.
