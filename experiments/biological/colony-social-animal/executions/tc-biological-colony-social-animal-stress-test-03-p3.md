# Execution 03 — Problem 3: Scarcity-Driven Tension in Social Unit

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does scarcity create real contour tension at social-unit level?**

## Decision
Yes. Resource scarcity produces explicit group-level trade-offs among maintenance, breeding, and adaptation.

## Case ID
BIO-3-003

## Biological context
Ant colony under prolonged food shortage.

## Unit boundary
Single colony.

## Time horizon
Late growth phase through stress phase.

## Environment
Low food availability, inter-colony competition, thermal stress.

## Resource constraints
Food influx, worker energy, brood-care capacity, nest defense effort.

## Observed demands
Worker maintenance, brood reduction/maintenance decisions, foraging strategy shifts.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: worker maintenance and nest defense.
- Reproduction: brood production and colony expansion.
- Evolution: persistent foraging/network reconfiguration improving future resilience.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (colony task allocation logic), Boundary (nest and colony membership), Gate (brood provisioning prioritization), Signal (pheromone and scarcity cues), Receiver (worker castes with thresholded response).

## Mediators
Competition intensity, colony age structure, temperature regime.

## Multi-unit dynamics observed
Compensation: specific worker cohorts absorb elevated mortality to protect colony-level function.

## What supports TC
- Scarcity triggers measurable reproductive suppression and defensive prioritization.
- Allocation posture changes over time, not as one-off events.

## What challenges TC
- Short abundance pulses can temporarily hide ongoing tension.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** scarcity-driven contour tension.
