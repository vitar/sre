# Execution 04 — Problem 4: Structural Element Mapping

## Scope
Problem tested: **Can Code/Boundary/Gate/Signal/Receiver map without metaphor inflation?**

## Decision
Yes; population ecology provides concrete mappings with diagnostic utility.

## Case ID
BIO-2-004

## Biological context
Fragmented amphibian metapopulation with variable hydroperiod habitats.

## Unit boundary
Metapopulation across connected wetlands.

## Time horizon
15 years.

## Environment
Hydrologic variability, pathogen exposure, land-use fragmentation.

## Resource constraints
Breeding sites, dispersal connectivity, juvenile survival habitat.

## Observed demands
Persistence of local subpopulations, reproduction, adaptive occupancy shifts.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: local persistence through unfavorable years.
- Reproduction: successful breeding across sites.
- Evolution: occupancy/trait strategy changes increasing future network viability.

## Structural elements mapping
- Code: inherited/learned breeding timing and habitat-use logic.
- Boundary: subpopulation and metapopulation membership rules.
- Gate: corridor permeability, hydroperiod suitability, reproductive barriers.
- Signal: rainfall timing, pathogen cues, density signals.
- Receiver: subpopulations with thresholded response capacity.

## Mediators
Connectivity quality, disturbance regime, disease dynamics.

## What supports TC
- Gate changes (corridor loss/recovery) strongly alter contour outcomes.
- Signal presence alone is insufficient if receiver legibility is low.

## What challenges TC
- Boundary porosity requires careful definition to avoid arbitrary unit drift.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** strong structural mapping at population scale.
