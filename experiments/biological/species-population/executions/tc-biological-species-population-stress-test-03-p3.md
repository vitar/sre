# Execution 03 — Problem 3: Scarcity and Contour Tension

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does scarcity create real contour tension?**

## Decision
Yes. Scarcity repeatedly forces reallocations among persistence, reproduction, and adaptation.

## Case ID
BIO-2-003

## Biological context
Coastal fish population under declining prey base and warming waters.

## Unit boundary
Managed stock subpopulation.

## Time horizon
12 recruitment cycles.

## Environment
Prey decline, temperature anomalies, competition shift.

## Resource constraints
Food density, spawning habitat quality, juvenile refuge.

## Observed demands
Adult survival, spawning output, phenological adjustment.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: reducing adult mortality and maintaining biomass floor.
- Reproduction: spawning effort and larval recruitment.
- Evolution: timing/range-use shifts for future viability.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (life-history schedule), Boundary (stock unit), Gate (spawning-ground access), Signal (temperature/prey cues), Receiver (age-structured cohorts).

## Mediators
Density dependence, fishing pressure context, migration bottlenecks.

## What supports TC
- Food scarcity decreases fecundity and alters survival strategy.
- Environmental shocks trigger measurable multi-contour reallocation.

## What challenges TC
- Management intervention can mask natural tension signatures.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports**: scarcity generates repeated contour competition.
