# Execution 06 — Problem 6: Mediators at Population Level

## Scope
Problem tested: **Are mediators visible and necessary at this level?**

## Decision
Yes. Cross-cutting ecological/social structures mediate allocation without becoming extra contours.

## Case ID
BIO-2-006

## Biological context
Same species in two regions with different mating systems and predator pressure.

## Unit boundary
Two comparable regional populations.

## Time horizon
6 generations.

## Environment
Similar climate/food baseline; different social structure and predation.

## Resource constraints
Mate access, safe territory, dispersal opportunity.

## Observed demands
Persistence, reproductive strategy, adaptive repositioning.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
Survival/Reproduction/Evolution held constant as conceptual contours; mediator differences explain posture shifts.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (shared species-level baseline + local variation), Boundary (regional populations), Gate (mating and dispersal filters), Signal (predator/density cues), Receiver (population subgroups).

## Mediators
Mating system type, territorial hierarchy, predator regime.

## What supports TC
- Same species shows distinct contour allocation under mediator changes.
- Mediators alter signal salience and gate behavior without adding new contour.

## What challenges TC
- Mediator concept can become generic ecology unless causal role in allocation is specified.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** mediator necessity and non-contour status.
