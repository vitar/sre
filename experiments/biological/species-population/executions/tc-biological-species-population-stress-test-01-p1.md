# Execution 01 — Problem 1: Population Boundary Legitimacy

## Scope
Problem tested: **Is the unit boundary legitimate at species/population scale?**

## Decision
A **local population in a declared habitat** is the most defensible initial unit; species-wide aggregation obscures real allocation differences.

## Case ID
BIO-2-001

## Biological context
Songbird population in fragmented temperate habitat with variable food and predation.

## Unit boundary
Local breeding population (single valley system).

## Time horizon
8 generations.

## Environment
Patchy habitat, seasonal food pulses, rising nest predation, moderate disease pressure.

## Resource constraints
Food, nesting territory, safe corridors, mate access.

## Observed demands
Population persistence, reproductive throughput, dispersal adaptation.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: reducing mortality and maintaining viable local abundance.
- Reproduction: offspring production and recruitment.
- Evolution: strategy/trait shifts improving future capability under fragmentation.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (population trait architecture), Boundary (local breeding membership), Gate (dispersal and mating barriers), Signal (predator/food/season cues), Receiver (subgroups with differential response).

## Mediators
Habitat fragmentation, corridor quality, density effects.

## What supports TC
- Boundary choice materially changes diagnosis (local vs species-wide).
- Resource flows and trade-offs are observable at local-population level.

## What challenges TC
- Frequent dispersal can blur boundary membership unless criteria are explicit.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** with explicit boundary declaration rules.
