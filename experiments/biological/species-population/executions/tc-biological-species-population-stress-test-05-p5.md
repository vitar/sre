# Execution 05 — Problem 5: Discipline of TC Evolution at Population Scale

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does TC Evolution fit better here, and can it remain narrow?**

## Decision
Yes, when restricted to persistent capability-changing shifts under constraint, not all long-term change.

## Case ID
BIO-2-005

## Biological context
Urban-adjacent bird population with persistent behavioral and niche-use shift.

## Unit boundary
City-edge subpopulation.

## Time horizon
20 years.

## Environment
Novel food sources, chronic noise, altered predator guild.

## Resource constraints
Nesting sites, safe foraging windows, reproductive success in disturbed habitat.

## Observed demands
Current persistence, annual breeding output, long-term niche adaptation.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: persistence in disturbed urban-edge ecology.
- Reproduction: successful nesting and fledging rates.
- Evolution: persistent strategy/trait shift changing future option space.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (population response repertoire), Boundary (subpopulation demography), Gate (habitat/admission filters), Signal (anthropogenic and ecological cues), Receiver (subgroups with differential sensitivity).

## Mediators
Disturbance pattern, social learning pathways, predation regime.

## What supports TC
- Not all changes count; only durable capability shifts qualify as Evolution.
- Some populations persist with little transformation, separating Survival from Evolution.

## What challenges TC
- Heavy overlap with standard evolutionary vocabulary can blur TC-specific value.

## Confidence
Medium-high

## Verdict
**Partially supports** with strict inclusion criteria for Evolution.
