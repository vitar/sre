# Execution 07 — Problem 7: Time and Multi-Generation Dynamics

## Scope
Problem tested: **Does time behave as TC requires?**

## Decision
Yes. Multi-generation sequence changes interpretation; snapshot views miss debt and delayed transitions.

## Case ID
BIO-2-007

## Biological context
Population that appears stable short-term before abrupt decline under prolonged climate stress.

## Unit boundary
Local population with long-term census data.

## Time horizon
18 generations.

## Environment
Gradual warming, intermittent drought, periodic disease outbreaks.

## Resource constraints
Breeding habitat persistence, food reliability, juvenile survival conditions.

## Observed demands
Short-term persistence, continued recruitment, adaptive response capacity.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: maintaining numbers in the near term.
- Reproduction: sustaining recruitment rates.
- Evolution: accumulating adaptive shifts for future conditions.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (trait distribution trajectory), Boundary (population continuity), Gate (habitat access thresholds), Signal (climate/disease cues), Receiver (generation-specific responsiveness).

## Mediators
Climate variability regime, disease intensity cycles, connectivity shifts.

## What supports TC
- Apparent stability masked accumulating vulnerability.
- Delayed collapse followed prolonged distortion/stress period.

## What challenges TC
- Requires longitudinal evidence quality; sparse data weakens inference.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** strong temporal constitutiveness at population scale.
