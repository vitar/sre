# Execution 02 — Problem 2: Contour Distinguishability

## Scope
Problem tested: **Are Survival, Reproduction, Evolution distinguishable at this scale?**

## Decision
Yes, by primary function; overlap exists but does not erase distinction.

## Case ID
BIO-2-002

## Biological context
Ungulate population under high winter mortality and variable fecundity.

## Unit boundary
Regional population.

## Time horizon
10 years (~4–5 generations).

## Environment
Harsh winters, predator pressure, periodic forage shortages.

## Resource constraints
Forage biomass, thermal refuge, breeding opportunities.

## Observed demands
Overwinter persistence, calf production, adaptive shift in seasonal movement.

## Proposed TC contour mapping
- Survival: overwinter maintenance and mortality control.
- Reproduction: birth rate and juvenile recruitment.
- Evolution: persistent shift in migration/rut timing improving future fitness options.

## Structural elements mapping
Code (heritable/learned seasonal strategy), Boundary (regional herd system), Gate (movement corridors, breeding access), Signal (climate and predator cues), Receiver (age/sex cohorts with differential response).

## Mediators
Predator regime, snowpack variability, social hierarchy.

## What supports TC
- Survival and reproduction trade-offs are explicit under severe winters.
- Long-term timing shifts are not reducible to immediate fecundity changes.

## What challenges TC
- Some adaptive shifts mix genetic and behavioral components, complicating clean categorization.

## Confidence
High

## Verdict
**Supports** contour separation in strong cases.
