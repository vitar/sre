Environmental Pressure — AI Industry TC Postures
The AI industry is the environmental shock in the labor market model — not a single actor but a composed multi-unit system. Each entity has a distinct TC posture that shapes the intensity, distribution, and addressability of the displacement pressure. No entity has a Code that intrinsically values labor market adaptation. The critical variable is therefore not intent but the regulatory mediator's capacity to redirect their output.

Shared Code — All Six
Maximize capability deployment. All six units share this as deep Code regardless of narrative differences. No unit has a Code that intrinsically values labor market stability.
None of the six invest in the labor market's adaptation infrastructure. Their E-contour investment is directed at AI capability — which is environmental pressure for the labor system, not relief.
Anthropic's safety orientation is directed at existential/catastrophic risk — not displacement-rate management. It is the closest to labor awareness; it is still structurally insufficient.
Critical Mediator — Regulation
Current configuration: low resolution (sector level, not task level), smooth selectivity, dynamic but lagging temporal behavior. Structurally mismatched to displacement speed.
The regulatory mediator is the key variable determining Branch A vs B — not any individual company's intent. AI entity Codes will not change organically.
Branch A requires mediator resolution to drop to task level and selectivity to sharpen — redirecting AI industry R-output toward labor market E-investment via fiscal or structural instruments.
Systemic Code–Narrative Divergence
Every entity has a narrative-Code gap on labor impact. Microsoft's is most visible given enterprise positioning. OpenAI's is most extreme in scope — AGI abundance narrative vs. near-term displacement Code.
Branch A does not emerge from within the AI industry. It requires an external mediator that is not currently configured to deliver it.
Both branches begin with identical AI industry postures. The divergence is determined entirely by whether the regulatory mediator reconfigures before the carrier population crosses the irreversibility threshold.

Events
2012ImageNet breakthrough — deep learning inflection. AI capability curve separates from prior trajectory. Labor market unaffected. E-debt begins accumulating below detection threshold. Human capital buffer intact.
2017Automation reports peak. 47% of US jobs at high risk. Distortion visible in research — not yet in allocation. NVIDIA, Google, OpenAI all scaling capability. No entity redirects resources toward labor adaptation. Borrowed Life phase active.
2020COVID accelerates digital displacement. Meta's open-source strategy and OpenAI's GPT-3 diffuse capability rapidly. Adaptation gap widens. Human capital buffer depleting faster than expected.
2022ChatGPT. Signal crosses Gate — white-collar displacement publicly legible. Microsoft Copilot, Google Workspace AI, OpenAI API activate simultaneously. Stress phase begins. Regulatory mediator begins reconfiguring.
2024Broad displacement: legal, financial analysis, content, coding. No AI entity Code contains labor adaptation investment. Overhead rises — incumbent protection activates. Carrier population approaching critical zone. Fork approaching.
2027Branch A: Regulatory mediator resolution drops to task level. Fiscal instruments redirect AI industry R-output toward adaptation infrastructure. Retraining scales. E-allocation rises. Carrier population stabilizes above irreversibility.
2027Branch B: Regulatory mediator remains mismatched — resolution too coarse. AI industry Code continues unredirected. Carrier population crosses irreversibility. Protection overhead locks in. Reinforcing loop activates.
2030Flow. New labor market equilibrium. Adaptation costs high and unevenly distributed — but the system retains Evolution capacity. E-debt closing. Human capital replenishing through retraining pipelines.
2030Starvation. Reinforcing loop: adaptation gap → protection overhead → less E-allocation → faster gap growth. Self-sustaining. Code-level intervention required — credential reform, institutional redesign, forced decoupling.
